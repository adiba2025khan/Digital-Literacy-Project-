In a notable real-world situation, a major communication breakdown occurred between a manager and an employee over a brief digital message. The manager sent a late-evening email simply stating, "We need to talk first thing tomorrow," without further context.
Because digital text lacks non-verbal cues like tone and facial expressions, the employee spent the night in a state of high anxiety, assuming they were about to be fired. In reality, the manager only wanted to offer praise for a recent project and discuss a promotion. This poor communication caused unnecessary emotional distress and a total loss of productivity for the employee that evening.
To prevent this, the manager should have practiced emotional proofreading by adding a brief, clarifying phrase such as, "Great work on the report; I’d like to discuss some exciting next steps tomorrow". Providing context or using a richer communication channel, like a quick phone call, would have ensured the intended positive tone was accurately conveyed.
