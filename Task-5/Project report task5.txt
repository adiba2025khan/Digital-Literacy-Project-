What surprised me most during this research was the sheer psychological sophistication of "Digital Arrest" scams currently targeting Indian youth. I previously assumed cybercrime was mostly technical—hacking or malware—but discovering that fraudsters spend hours on video calls impersonating CBI or police officers to emotionally manipulate students into "security deposits" was a revelation. It highlights that our greatest vulnerability isn't our software, but our inherent trust in authority figures and the fear of legal trouble.
As a result, the one habit I am personally changing is my approach to public Wi-Fi hygiene. I used to routinely check my bank balance or approve quick UPI payments while on campus or café Wi-Fi. Moving forward, I will strictly use mobile data for any financial activity and have disabled "Auto-Join" for public networks on my phone to prevent accidental data exposure to "Man-in-the-Middle" attacks.
