Managing your digital footprint now is basically "future-proofing" your career. Here is a quick checklist to help you stay responsible while still enjoying your social life.

The Do’s 
Do set your profiles to private: Regularly audit your privacy settings to ensure only friends see your personal posts 
Do think before you post: Ask yourself if you’d be comfortable with a future employer or a grandmother seeing the content 
Do network professionally: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professors, and industry leaders 
Do fact-check information: Verify news or academic claims before sharing them to avoid spreading misinformation 
Do use strong, unique passwords: Protect your accounts from being hacked or used to post unauthorised content
The Don’ts 
Don’t post illegal or risky activity: Avoid sharing photos involving underage drinking, drug use, or reckless behavior
Don’t engage in cyberbullying: Never use your platform to harass, shame, or bully classmates or faculty .
Don’t share sensitive data: Keep your student ID number, home address, and current exact location private .
Don’t vent about your school or job: Complaints about professors or internships can quickly get back to the people in charge .
Don’t overshare: Avoid "dumping" personal drama or private conflicts that you might regret once the emotions fade.
